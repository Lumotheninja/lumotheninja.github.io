Inspecting streaming sites using Chrome web inspector

on Video streaming, Reverse engineering

What actually goes on when you watch a video

We have all done it at some point in our lives, where we watched online movies for free on websites like putlocker, 123movies etc. The basics of video streaming sites are shown in this article on [zippia](https://www.zippia.com/advice/streaming-sites-are-they-illegal-how-do-they-make-money/). As a recap, streaming sites often do not host their own content, but rather offer a search service to access these types of content on 3rd party hosts. The legality of these websites are grey but they still get served DMCA notices. Users seems to be off the hook if they do not download these movies or organize a big watch party. These websites have 2 types of ads: 1. banner ads, which are displayed on their websites and 2. video ads, which are embedded within the video itself. However, I would like to investigate further, in particular the behaviour of these websites. They seem to have new tabs that opens on click and disable right click. So lets try to understand the more technical questions: 1. How are these websites designed? 2. What requests are the websites making? 3. What are the risks of watching a video streamed this way? # How are these websites designed? So lets imagine this scenario: you Googled a movie title, and a website offers to stream it for free. You enter the website and click on the video. A new tab opens, it links to a VPN site, you closed it. There is some buffering, and the video starts to play. I have tried out the steps above by Googling "Avengers 2012 watch free" and got into a streaming website. ## HTML Elements So first of all, we can do a simple inspect element, and we will find out that the video is embedded in an iframe. For example, a random monetized video from Youtube can be embedded with an iframe tag (this monetized part will be important later): ~~~javascript ~~~ When you embed another website through an iFrame, it loads any HTML/CSS/JS from the embedded website within your website. It is similar to embedding a Youtube video. If you embed a video with ads from Youtube, it loads all of Youtube's HTML/CSS/JS and the video's ads will be displayed as well, as per [Youtube's policy](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/132596?hl=en). However, Youtube's embedded video is definitely more benign, its just showing ads. The website that is embedded on streaming sites might be doing much more. ## Scripts So after looking at the HTML elements, lets look at the scripts. In this case, the scripts are minified so we are unable to understand them, but we can understand them through the behaviour. If the browser opens a new tab to another website (might even be legit websites) when you click on the video, it means that you have clicked on an ad. Most modern browsers nowadays block popups that are not requested, but in this case, you requested it ever since you click play on the movie, according to [javascript.info](https://javascript.info/popup-windows) ~~~javascript // popup blocked window.open('https://javascript.info'); // popup allowed button.onclick = () => { window.open('https://javascript.info'); }; ~~~ _Source: javascript.info_ Sometimes they will even outright ban right click altogether on the video. For those websites, the aim is simple, don't let your viewers/competitors download your souce by right clicking "save video as". Youtube does this as well. # What requests are the websites making? Using Google Chrome console, we can further see the requests that the website is making. There are 5 requests in this example, one of them an ad request from popcash.net. If I start my adblock extension on chrome, this popcash.net request will be blocked. 5 requests: ![5 requests](/assets/img/blog/5requests.jpg) 4 requests now after adblock is activated: ![4 requests](/assets/img/blog/4requests.jpg) One thing to pay attention here is the picture with 5 requests. As you can see, the origin of this website is not the same one as I started but rather the embedded website, which means that the request is being made from the embedded site. # What are the risks of watching a video streamed this way? According to cybersecurity experts, some malicious websites are able to harm your computer when you browse it by using exploit kits (but the articles are from a long time ago and they concern the use of Adobe flash player which is long known to be unsafe), so it is possible that your computer will be harmed. However, if those exploits still exists and not patched, it will be dangerous on any website, not just limited to illegal streaming sites. And we also know their motives, they are just like Youtube except they don't honour copyrights. Given the possibility that any website might be harmful and we know their motive, t is up to you to figure out whether you want to take the risk.
